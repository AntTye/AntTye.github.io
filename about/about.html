Here's a little bit about me.

Where I’m from

I’m from the East Coast, just south of Boston, Massachusetts. Throughout my life, I’ve traveled with my family, who are first-generation immigrants. My family has been incredibly supportive of me on this journey, and I’m dedicated to helping them in any way I can. By pursuing higher education, I aim to achieve what they couldn’t—leveraging education to build a strong career and future.

What I do now

I’m currently a CSE undergraduate focused on full-stack software development and cloud services. Alongside my studies, I work on side projects to explore new tools and refine my skills. In my downtime, I enjoy spending time with my family and friends or going to the gym, which is one of my biggest passions. Recently, in November 2024, I competed in a powerlifting competition, which was a club planned competition for the University of Connecticut.

Where I’m at now

I’m at a pivotal point in my life where I’m seriously considering life after college and what would be best for both me and my family, who have supported me unconditionally. Currently, I live off-campus, about 15 minutes from UConn’s Storrs campus, sharing a house with friends while doing my best to succeed academically and prepare for the future.

What I used to do

Previously, I interned at a startup called Antisocial, where I discovered my passion for creating interactive software that directly engages users. It was a summer internship lasting three months, but it significantly boosted my understanding and confidence in software development. Before that, I worked in various roles, including concierge, lifeguarding, and being the host at my family-owned restaurant. These experiences taught me the value of hard work and adaptability.
